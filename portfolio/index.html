Concentrix India Private Limited
Standarization of Call Handling

    Concentrix are a leader in BPO sectors and specialise in handling customer and technical support for high profile clients. Operations were dealing with the high call cycle time and reduced customer satisfaction rate. Working as a data analyst, I was assigned with the task to find out what are the shortfalls and provide sustainable data driven solution.
    I approached the problem from the ground up. First, by hearing live calls to understand different sections of the call; How a call is started, how an issue is approached, troubleshooting and call closing.
After understanding the call workflow, we dove into the historic data focusing on few key parameters,
 1. Call length.
 2. First Time Resolution (FTR).
 3. Survey Return Rates on both positive and Negative responses.
 4. Technical calls vs non-technical calls.
When I analysed a sub set of the historic data with the above key parameters, the below conclusion were drawn,
 1. Shorter calls resulted in more FTR and a happy customer.
 2. Collecting customer data took time in the call opening and added up to 30% of call length.
 3. Survey Return rates was directly proportional to FTR and less time spent on call.
 4. 90% of callers who are calling for the 3rd or 4th time for the same issue gave negative survey 100% of the time.

    Improvements were made to the process which included training to improve technical and soft skills of the advisor, IVR was automated to collect the customer information reducing the call length, FTR or STR was given more importance to reduce the negative survey.
    Improvements are evident from day 1, as the positive survey rate increased from 80% to 95%, call length reduced which in turn translated to more calls attended in less time, free time for the associates due to dry queue.

    Subsequent projects which introduced call ownership for repeat callers, mandatory monthly skill assessment, standardised call flow and questioning techniques further increased Quality, SLA and Over KPI’s of the process.
